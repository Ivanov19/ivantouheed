Undergraduate Researcher · Supervisor: Dr. Imtiaz Ahmed

Research interests Machine learning for materials discovery Nanomaterials synthesis & characterization Energy storage materials Nanoelectronics Nanophotonics Micro/nano-engineered devices

Current Work — Machine-Learning-Guided Electrode Design for Supercapacitors: From Data Mining to Experimental Validation. I am building a structured literature dataset of oxide-based electrode materials (perovskites, spinels, layered oxides, nitrides, and oxynitrides) and applying machine learning to map material and process descriptors — synthesis method, calcination conditions, electrolyte, BET surface area, morphology, and crystal phase — to specific capacitance (Csp), with SHAP-based feature-importance analysis to identify the dominant descriptors governing performance.

Model-shortlisted candidates are then validated experimentally: ML-identified high-impact features guide targeted material synthesis, followed by structural characterization (BET, XRD, Raman, XPS) and electrochemical testing (CV, GCD, EIS) — closing the loop between data-driven prediction and physical material behavior.
