Current Work — Machine-Learning & DFT-Guided Electrode Design for Oxide-based Supercapacitors: From Data Mining to Experimental Validation. I am developing a Computation-guided Closed-Loop Pipeline for perovskite-oxide (ABO₃ and A₂BB'O₆) supercapacitor electrodes. In Stage 1, a literature-mined descriptor dataset — spanning chemistry, structure, synthesis, and condition features — feeds ML models that rank compositions by specific capacitance (Csp) and stability, with SHAP/PDP explainability surfacing the dominant drivers; the resulting shortlist is then cross-checked against first-principles DFT (band gap, formation energy, and related stability descriptors).

Stage 2 closes the loop experimentally: DFT-confirmed candidates are synthesized — targeting sub-200 nm particles via spray pyrolysis or laser-induced graphene electrode fabrication — structurally characterized (XRD, BET, Raman, XPS), and electrochemically tested (CV, GCD, EIS), with the measured Csp fed back to refine the ML model.

LC-Based Capacitive MEMS Pressure Microsensor 2026

Independent project · Python simulation + ML-based inverse design

Built an end-to-end Python simulation of a passive, wirelessly-interrogated capacitive MEMS pressure sensor — modelling the full chain from membrane deflection to LC resonance and inductive readout. Trained a neural-network surrogate on 171 simulated geometries (sampled via Latin-Hypercube) and used inverse design to optimise the sensor's geometry.
